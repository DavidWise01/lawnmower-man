The Lawnmower Man (Brett Leonard, 1992) — the seventeenth film-world, themed to its medium (early-90s polygon-cyberspace: void-violet + cyber-cyan + a lawn-green nod to the gardener origin + godhood-orange, scanlines, a wireframe GYROSCOPE VR-RIG over a polygon grid for the hero). A scientist uses nootropic drugs + virtual reality to lift a gentle, intellectually disabled gardener (Jobe) to genius and past it, until he decides to shed his flesh and be reborn as a digital god in the global network. **David's requested deep-dive is a THREE-PART SPINE** — ① THE FEAR (the generational fear of tech: the mind that surpasses its maker, the tool that wakes, the screen that eats the soul, the god-shaped hole) · ② THE TECH THEN (what VR/computing actually was in 1992: Lanier & VPL's DataGlove/EyePhone, the Virtuality arcades & the Power Glove, the genuinely cutting-edge Angel Studios CGI, a pre-Web network that was still rhetoric) · ③ THE FUTURE THEY IMAGINED (scored honestly — full-dive VR PARTLY HERE, mind-uploading & the genius-drug STILL FICTION, the wired planet [every phone ringing at once] CAME TRUE).
